Ms. Entrepreneur

A social media platform that allows users to connect with and empower one another, find upcoming and nearby events, explore different businesses that they can support, and develop a better sense on how to manage their own companies.



Background

This project was developed in June of 2021 during the virtual Girls Who Code Work Prep program (in collaboration with JPMorgan Chase) with two other UX designers, with a timeline of 2 hours.

Participants were separated into groups that focused on different challenges, and our group was assigned to target the following issue: "Female business owners often work 3X harder to build relationships and seek investment funding as compared to their male counterparts. How can we use technology to to help women get access to these networks and build relationships / share tools that will help them be successful in securing funding?". Throughout this buildathon, we implemented our newly learned skills around Agile Methodology and UX Design to to create a technical solution for this social good challenge around a female driven issue.



My Role

I was n UX Designer in the project cycle, and I led my group by planning the wireframe and coordinating brainstorming sessions amongst two other designers in my group. I facilitated conversations to ensure that our product would stand out while remaining inclusive, and I created a slide deck to present our product in front of judges and our peers in the program.



Project Summary

To start, we spent time in brainstorming sessions discussing possible solutions to this problem. The main issue was the lack of access to existing networks for women to build relationships from, so we considered our personal experiences from building virtual relationships. This led us to reference platforms like LinkedIn and Handshake, which often help users connected with one another and also provided us with a foundation for what we wanted our product to look like. However, we wanted an even more advanced version of these applications with features that allowed specifically women and non-binary individuals (who are also underrepresented) to feel more comfortable sharing their stories with one another. As a result, we designed a social media platform that primarily supports the interactions between women and non-binary individuals with the following features: allows users to connect with and empower one another, find upcoming and nearby events, explore different businesses that they can support, and develop a better sense on how to manage their own companies. This includes the following features:


  • A home page that displays upcoming events for users to connect with one another at and posts that users can interact with one another through.
  • A calendar that also functions as a schedule so that users can easily identify upcoming events. There is an attached notes section at the bottom of the calendar for users to record reminders or any meaningful topics from events.
  • An explore page for users to discover new events, people, and/or hashtags that automatically provides suggestions. They can also find specific items (e.g. users or topics of interest) with the search bar at the top of the page.
  • A messaging page that allows users to continue conversations through our platform, allowing them a convenient way to ask each other questions related to their businesses.
  • A profile page that provides more insight on who business owners are and shares the experiences that inspired owners to start their business. This helps users learn about the human behind a business, nurturing greater and stronger connections.

    • To be even more inclusive, we wanted to make sure that everyone who wanted to support these underrepresented groups could participate if interested. In this manner, our application allows men to sign up as investors for the registered businesses.


    All in all, we hoped to create an application that is fun and relaxing for our users while they developed a brand and built a successful business. We also wanted to ensure that our model encouraged people to invest in the person -- not just a company. With the implementation of profile pages that narrates the story of a person behind their business and with the limitless opportunities for user interactions, we believe that we were able to accomplish this vision.

    What I Learned

    This was my first project with Figma, so I learned a lot through this hands-on experience! I greatly enjoyed the design process, as well as collaborating with my peers to decide on how we would develop our final product. This project also reinforced the importance of creating projects that can help our communities, as we were reminded that we can use our extensive experience with certain technologies like social media for good use. Another important component that we were reminded to consider is including the factors that will generate the most impact for our target audience. There were already many extisting iterations of the same category of product (e.g. Handshake could be characterized as an alternative form of LinkedIn, since both are used for job opportunities), so we had to conceptualize features that set our application apart. I found this process to be extremely exciting, as it allowed us to channel our creative energies, and we were able to consolidate all of our ideas together.


    Although we were not able to fully execute our ideas due to the time constraint of the buildathon, our group's skill set grew tremendously from the start of the session. We learned and taught each other how to navigate through Figma, and our habit of collaborating with one another was a significant component that helped us win our challenge's category. I was very excited to have created my first UX project, and I am confident in my ability to grow even more from this point forward.
